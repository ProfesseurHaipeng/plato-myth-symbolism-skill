# Deep-learning path

Use these modules in order. Every module produces a reviewable artifact rather than a free-floating interpretation.

1. **Locate** — find a passage by dialogue and Stephanus number; produce a source card with translation and license note.
2. **Frame** — identify speaker, audience, narrative nesting, dramatic situation, and preceding/following argument.
3. **Map** — express the philosophical problem, image/narrative, analytical function, and unresolved point using an argument-map record.
4. **Read concepts** — compare a verified Greek term, translation choices, and ambiguity without inventing Greek quotations.
5. **Compare interpretations** — contrast epistemic, political, ethical, literary, and reception-focused readings; state what evidence each uses and misses.
6. **Audit reception** — mark Neoplatonic, Christian, psychoanalytic, archaeological, or popular uses as later reception.
7. **Synthesize** — answer in the six-part evidence/context/concepts/readings/limits/next-step format.

Pass condition: a reader can locate the passage, distinguish its dramatic voice from Plato's final position, name at least two defensible readings, and see why the text does not settle a modern empirical claim.
